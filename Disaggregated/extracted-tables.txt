=== TABLE ===
Property | Effect
VRF AZLOCAL has no default route | VMs cannot accidentally route to the internet — north-south requires an explicit route leak at the service leaf
Underlay VRF has no default route | Switch-to-switch communication cannot be redirected externally — prevents route poisoning
Spine sees only underlay VRF | A compromised spine cannot inspect or inject workload traffic
No route leaking on compute leaves | Compute leaves only participate in VRF AZLOCAL — no external routes are present

=== TABLE ===
Question | Answer
Can Azure Local traffic accidentally leak to the internet? | No — it’s locked inside VRF AZLOCAL. Internet access requires a deliberate route leak on the service leaf or an integrated firewall/load balancer. Compute leaves have no external routes.
Can a switch management issue take down VM traffic? | No — different VRFs, different routing tables. A BGP misconfiguration in the underlay doesn’t touch workload traffic.
How does a VM in Rack 1 talk to a VM in Rack 3? | East-west: Leaf-1 wraps the frame in VXLAN, routes it across the spine to Leaf-3, which unwraps it. All within VRF AZLOCAL.
How does a VM reach the internet? | North-south: The compute leaf VXLAN-wraps the traffic, spine transits it to the service leaf, which decapsulates and route-leaks from AZLOCAL to the DC core. The compute leaf and spine never see external routes.
Can VLAN 7 talk to VLAN 6? | Yes — both are in VRF AZLOCAL. Routing happens locally on the leaf switch via SVIs (Anycast Gateway). The spine is only involved if the hosts are in different racks.
Do all racks share the same VRF? | Yes — VRF AZLOCAL spans all leaf switches in the cluster.
Is VRF a VLAN? | No — VLANs separate traffic at Layer 2 (Ethernet). VRF separates traffic at Layer 3 (IP routing). VLANs are members of a VRF.
Why not just use firewall rules instead of VRFs? | Firewall rules can be misconfigured or removed. VRF isolation is structural — traffic physically cannot cross VRF boundaries without an explicit route leak configured on the switch.
